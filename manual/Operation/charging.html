Charging ​

The RBQ10 can be charged using either a charger or a docking station. The charger utilizes the charging port on top of the robot, while the docking station allows charging through the port underneath the robot. Alternatively, the docking station can be connected to a charging cable, which is then attached to the charging port on the upper part of the robot.

⚠️ Only charge the robot with the provided RBQ10 Power Supply or RBQ10 Docking Station.

Charging by External Charging Wire ​

Directly connect the external charging wire to the robot's upper charging terminal.

Manual Docking ​

Step 1: Position the robot's rear camera to face the docking station ​

⚠️ Ensure that the distance between the docking station and the robot is within the range of 1 to 3 meters.

Step 2: Press Docking button ​

💡 The docking process will automatically begin once the robot recognizes the QR code.

Docking Station Installation Guide ​

The charging station is waterproof,
but if there is water at the charging terminal,
it may cause charging issues.
Therefore, it’s recommended to place it in an area where water splashes are minimized.

The power wire is waterproof,
but when connecting to an external outlet,
take care to ensure that the plug connection is protected from water ingress.

Prolonged exposure to direct sunlight may lead to overheating,
so it's recommended to keep the charging station in the shaded area.

Fix the charging station with the floor using the fix hold of the base plate.
